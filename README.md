FitnessHub Subscription Analytics Dashboard
Project Overview

This Power BI dashboard analyzes customer subscription behavior for FitnessHub using cohort analysis, revenue forecasting, and customer acquisition cost (CAC) analysis. The objective is to identify customer retention patterns, forecast future subscription revenue, and evaluate how long it takes customer cohorts to generate enough lifetime value (LTV) to recover acquisition costs.

Business Questions
1. Customer Cohort Analysis
Business Question

After how many months do customers typically cancel their subscriptions?

Findings

The cohort analysis shows that customer churn is highest during the first three months after signup. Across nearly every monthly cohort, customer retention falls by approximately 50% within the first three months before declining at a much slower rate.

Customers who remain subscribed beyond 6–9 months show significantly higher retention, indicating that long-term subscribers are much less likely to cancel.

Business Insight

The first three months represent the highest-risk period in the customer lifecycle. Improving onboarding, customer engagement, and retention initiatives during this period could substantially increase customer lifetime value.

2. Revenue Forecast
Business Question

What is the expected subscription revenue over the next 12 months? Does the forecast show evidence of seasonality?

Findings

Historical subscription revenue demonstrates a strong upward trend from January 2024 through December 2025. The 12-month forecast projects continued revenue growth, increasing from approximately $200K per month at the beginning of the forecast to approximately $350K per month by the end of the forecast period.

The forecast does not display a recurring seasonal pattern. Instead, revenue is expected to continue increasing steadily, although forecast uncertainty widens further into the future.

Business Insight

The projected revenue growth suggests FitnessHub's subscription business is expanding consistently. Since the forecast shows little evidence of seasonality, sustained customer acquisition and retention efforts are likely to have a greater impact on future revenue than seasonal marketing campaigns.

3. CAC vs. LTV Analysis
Business Question

How long does it take for the 2024 and 2025 customer cohorts to recover Customer Acquisition Cost (CAC)?

Findings

The CAC vs. LTV analysis shows that both the 2024 and 2025 customer cohorts recover their acquisition costs after approximately 11 months. In both cohorts, cumulative customer lifetime value exceeds total customer acquisition cost near the end of the first year.

Business Insight

An approximately 11-month CAC payback period indicates that FitnessHub must retain customers for nearly one year before acquisition investments become profitable. Improving customer retention during the first year would shorten the payback period, improve marketing efficiency, and increase long-term profitability.

Key Insights
Customer churn is highest during the first three months after signup.
Customers who remain subscribed beyond 6–9 months are significantly more likely to stay long term.
Subscription revenue has grown consistently since January 2024.
The 12-month forecast projects monthly subscription revenue increasing to approximately $350K.
The forecast shows little evidence of seasonality, suggesting growth is driven primarily by customer acquisition and retention.
Both the 2024 and 2025 customer cohorts recover customer acquisition costs after approximately 11 months.
